# Maker comment — TabStyr

Hey everyone 👋

I built TabStyr because every "where did my time go?" tool I tried wanted an account, a cloud sync, and a quiet little pipe sending my browsing history to someone else's server. I wanted the opposite: real insight into my browser time, with **zero** of it ever leaving my machine.

So TabStyr is fully local. It measures the time you're *actively* on each tab and turns it into a clean dashboard — today vs. your weekly average, trends, an activity heatmap, a daily focus score, and automatic categories (Work, Dev, Social, Media, Shopping…) across hundreds of sites you can re-classify yourself. There's also a daily work log you can copy straight into a standup or invoice, and gentle nudges for the stale tabs you forgot about.

The privacy part isn't a feature line, it's the whole architecture:
- 100% local (IndexedDB), auto-pruned to a rolling 90 days
- No accounts, no servers, no analytics, no ads, no network requests
- It never reads page content — only the focused tab's URL + title
- Export, encrypt, or wipe everything in one click

It's free, works on Chrome and Chromium browsers, and speaks 11 languages.

I'd genuinely love your feedback — especially on the categories and what stats you'd want next. What would make this a daily-driver for you?
